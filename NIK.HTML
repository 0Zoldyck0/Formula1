Andreas Nikolaus "Niki" Lauda

   February 1949 – 20 May 2019) was an Austrian Formula One driver and aviation entrepreneur. He was a three-time F1 World Drivers' Champion, winning in 1975, 1977 and 1984, and is the only driver in F1 history to have been champion for both Ferrari and McLaren, two of the sport's most successful constructors.

   He was an aviation entrepreneur who founded and ran three airlines: Lauda Air, Niki and Lauda. He was also a consultant for Scuderia Ferrari and team manager of the Jaguar Formula One racing team for two years. Afterwards, he worked as a pundit for German TV during Grand Prix weekends and acted as non-executive chairman of Mercedes-AMG Petronas Motorsport, of which Lauda owned 10%.

   After a couple of years at Brabham and two years' hiatus, Lauda returned and raced four seasons for McLaren between 1982 and 1985, during which he won the 1984 title by half a point over his teammate Alain Prost. Lauda was seriously injured in a crash at the 1976 German Grand Prix while racing at the Nürburgring; during the crash his Ferrari 312T2 burst into flames nearly resulting in his death after inhaling hot toxic fumes and suffering severe burns. Lauda emerged as Formula One's star driver amid a 1975 title win and leading the 1976 championship battle.

He Gave Away His Trophies

   For a man who went on to have such a successful career in racing, it may be surprising to find out that he actually didn't hold on to a whole lot of his trophies. The reason why is even more surprising than you'd think. These trophies from many of his successful races were actually given away to his local garage in exchange for a lifetime supply of free car washes.

His Lasting Legacy

   As one of the greatest of all time, it's very hard to forget Niki Lauda and his legacy continues today, inspiring current and future drivers to take a chance at racing themselves. His greatness meant an introduction into the International Motorsports Hall of Fame in 1993 and on the 20th May 2019, Lauda passed away, leaving us with some great memories of his moments both on and off the track.
