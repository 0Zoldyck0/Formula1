> POINTS <

During an F1 Grand Prix, both drivers and teams earn points which go towards their score in the championship. Whichever team and driver has the highest total by the end of the year is named champion. The F1 World Championship consists of two distinct competitions. Drivers who finish in the top 10 in any given race score points for both themselves and their teams. Max Verstappen, who started the 2016 season with Toro Rosso, switched to Red Bull after four races. A team can achieve a maximum of 44 World Championship points per race. Each F1 team runs two cars and both are able to score points.

Lewis Hamilton currently holds the record for the most points scored by a driver, with 4405.5 points, largely due the increased number of points awarded since 2010. Michael Schumacher, who finished his career with 1566 points, held the record until mid-2013, when Fernando Alonso overtook him. Alberto Ascari and Jim Clark (twice) have scored 100% of the available points to them in a season, in times when not all scores counted. For constructors, Ferrari have scored the most points.
